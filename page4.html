Life at UBC Bionics

Join one of the fastest growing design teams at UBC! Our team is composed of some of the best and brightest science and engineering students, ranging from mechanical to biomedical engineering and computer science. Together, seize the opportunity to learn, grow, network, and utilize your engineering skills to become the best engineer you can be. Our team is a tight-knit group that engages in extracurricular activities. As they say, we enjoy the happy times and work through the hard ones, together. 

Our Positions

Administration

The administration sub-team is responsible for overseeing and managing the team's public affairs. They work securing sponsorship, maintaining the budget, and promoting the team. Additionally, they work on member recruitment, organizing meetings, planning events, and update the team's social media presence.

Apply
Electrical

The electrical sub-team is responsible for developing the electrical components into the prototype. Currently, they are working on EMG acquisition and battery management systems. They are developing a PCB using Altium that integrates sensors, motors, and actuators. A major part of their work includes the use of Oscilliscope for testing.

Apply
Mechanical

The mechanical sub-team designs the structure of the current project and models the devices on CAD software. They research and develop mechanical designs via engineering design process, prototyping, and SolidWorks. They're ultimately responsible for the printing and assembly of the prototype. 

Apply
Analytics Software

The analytics sub-team uses data science and machine learning to improve the usability of the current project. Part of their work includes programming with Python and performing data analysis on sensor information like EMG, FSR, etc. 

Apply
Embedded Software

The embedded systems sub-team is responsible for detailing the most optimal design, programming, and actuation methods to follow. They work on low-level control of the hardware and take in sensor data. Currently, they are programming with C++, but will move forward Rust.

Application FAQ

When can I apply?

The best time to apply is during our recruitment season, which occurs at the start of every winter session semester! However, our email inbox remains open throughout the year.

Will I be considered if I do not have prior experience being on a design team?                                  

Yes! We encourage all candidates interested in joining the team to send in an application, regardless of what your experience may include. We are ultimately looking for those who are willing to dedicate the time and energy to learn and further the goals of our team.

What sub-team should I apply for?

The final choice that you list on your application is up to you! All we ask is that you have an idea of which sub-team(s) aligns best with your own interests or studies, to better help us decide what the best fit is for you.
